Sashimi

Description

Sashimi is a Japanese dish of thinly-sliced raw food, usually fish and seafood, but also sometimes other meats.

Ingredients

  • Daikon radish about 4″ (10 cm)
  • Tuna Sashimi grade, block
  • Yellowtail Sashimi grade, fillet
  • Salmon Sashimi grade, block
  • Shiso leaves
  • Wasabi
  • Soy Sauce

Steps

  1. Remove skin of Daikon radish. Slice thinly with a mandolin or slicer. Stack 3-4 layers of thin Daikon, and julienne very thinly. Put it in a bowl of water and leave for 15 minutes to make it crispy. Strain and remove moisture.
  2. Slice tuna and yellowtail into 1/4″-1/3″ thick (6 mm-8 mm) pieces. Slice salmon thinly at a slight angle.
  3. Serve Sashimi on a plate with radish, Shiso leaves, and Wasabi along with Soy Sauce for dipping.
